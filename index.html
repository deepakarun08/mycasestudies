My UX Process

A process built around
understanding before designing.

I follow a user-centred design approach grounded in evidence, not assumption. Every project begins with understanding the problem deeply — through research, stakeholder alignment, and domain immersion — before a single frame is drawn. Iteration is baked in, not bolted on.

01 — Discover

Research & Empathy

User interviews, contextual inquiry, competitive analysis, and stakeholder workshops surface the real problem — not the assumed one. I map mental models and pain points before any solution thinking begins.

02 — Define

Synthesis & Framing

Research findings are synthesised into affinity maps, user journey maps, and clear "How Might We" statements — producing a shared design brief that aligns business goals with user needs.

03 — Design

Structure Before Pixels

Information architecture and interaction flows are validated before visual design begins. Wireframes expose structural problems cheaply. Fidelity increases only once the logic is sound.

04 — Validate

Test & Iterate

Prototypes are tested against real tasks with real users. Usability findings directly drive design revisions. This loop continues until the design is validated — not just stakeholder-approved.

05 — Deliver

Handoff & Advocacy

Annotated specs and component documentation ensure what gets built matches what was designed. I stay involved through development — reviewing builds and advocating at every sprint.

Design Philosophy

Good UX is invisible.
Bad UX isn't.

I design for the user who has 30 seconds, a deadline, and no patience for unclear interfaces. Enterprise users aren't a different species — they have the same cognitive limitations as anyone else, but higher stakes when things go wrong. Clarity, predictability, and reduced cognitive load sit at the centre of every design decision I make.

My goal is never to make something look impressive in a demo. It's to make something work so well that nobody notices the design — only the outcome.

Principle 01

Structure follows cognition, not convention

Layout and IA should mirror how users actually think about their task — not how the business organises its data internally. I map user mental models before I draw a single layout.

Principle 02

Constraints are design inputs, not obstacles

WCAG standards, design system rules, technical limitations — these aren't restrictions. They're the context inside which good design happens. Working with constraints produces sharper decisions.

Principle 03

Multi-role doesn't mean multiple products

Complex systems with many user roles need a single adaptive shell — not separate applications. I design the shared structure first, then the role-specific differences. Not the other way around.

Principle 04

Documentation is the final act of design thinking

A design that isn't fully specified isn't finished. Annotation, edge case documentation, and handoff clarity are part of the design process — and I treat them that way.
